DjangoCon.US 2016

DjangoCon US is here! We are in Hunstman Hall, 3730 Walnut Street

DjangoCon is an international community conference for the community by the community, held each year in North America, about the Django web framework.

We’ve got something for everyone, from the person who develops Django applications for a living to the person who just tinkers in their spare time. DjangoCon is a great place to learn, exchange ideas, and meet great people. Our team is making sure that participating in DjangoCon is easy for you!
Read more about how we’re making the conference accessible and affordable.

DjangoCon US

Code of Conduct

We're committed to providing a safe and friendly environment for everyone to learn, meet, and enjoy the conference. All organizers, attendees, speakers and sponsors must follow the Code of Conduct.

Commitment to Diversity

It is a core aim of DjangoCon US 2016 to encourage diversity, enforce our Code of Conduct, and make our event as inclusive and accessible as possible. Read more in our diversity statement.

Financial Aid

As part of our commitment to diversity, we have funds to meet the cost of tickets, travel, and accommodation for attendees who need financial support. Applications closed on April 25.

Child care

We're proud to offer complimentary child care for kids 18 months through 6 years old! More information on how to sign your child up coming soon.

Speaker Mentors

We have speaker mentors available to help you before and after your talk is accepted! They can help you refine your proposal, organize your slides, and practice your talk.

Day 1 Orientation

Are you new to DjangoConUS or tech conferences in general? Are you worried you might feel out of place? Are you attending DjangoCon by yourself? If so, the Day 1 orientation is for you! We’ll help you get your bearings so you can have the BEST DjangoCon possible. More details coming in the blog.

Board Game Night

On Tuesday night, we'll be playing board games in Huntsman Hall at 6:00 pm on the 8th floor!

Program

The DjangoCon US program features both novice talks for people just starting their journey into the world of development, as well as advanced talks for experienced developers.

Venue accessibility

We’re aiming to make this event accessible for everyone. Read more about how we’re working to make DjangoCon US accessible for everyone, and let us know if we’ve missed something.

Quiet Room

We will provide a clearly-marked designated quiet room. This room is intended to be a calm and quiet place for anyone who needs to have a break from the bustle of the conference, for whatever reason.

Job Search

We’ll have boards available so you can let people know if you’re recruiting new team members, or looking for a new position yourself!

Dietary requirements

We aim to meet all dietary requirements. You will be able to specify these when purchasing your ticket.

Lightning Talks (aka ⚡️Talks)

Lightning Talks are short (5-minute maximum) presentations. Signups for ⚡️Talks will be available at DjangoCon. If you submitted a talk, but it wasn't accepted, consider doing a ⚡️Talk version. Or, if you've never presented at a conference, a ⚡️Talk can be a great way to get started.

Welcome to Philadelphia!

The birthplace of America is a perfect place to make your first commit, give your first talk, or attend your first Django conference! The Wharton School of Business is working with us to make this event accessible, fun, and delicious. Philadelphia’s rich history, amazing sports teams, quintessentially American cuisine, and classic architecture make it a perfect backdrop to a week spent learning about code. The City of Siblingly Love, and all the DjangoCon organizers, welcome you!
